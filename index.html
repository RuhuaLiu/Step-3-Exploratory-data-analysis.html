import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
import seaborn as sns

# Set the aesthetic style of the plots
sns.set_style("whitegrid")

# Convert the Date column to datetime format for time-based analysis
df['Date'] = pd.to_datetime(df['Date'])
df['Year'] = df['Date'].dt.year

# Initialize the figure
plt.figure(figsize=(20, 20))

# 1. Launch site vs. Success Rate
plt.subplot(3, 2, 1)
site_success_rate = df.groupby('LaunchSite')['LandingOutcomeLabel'].mean()
sns.barplot(x=site_success_rate.index, y=site_success_rate.values)
plt.title('Success Rate vs. Launch Site')
plt.ylabel('Success Rate')

# 2. Payload Mass Range vs. Launch Site
plt.subplot(3, 2, 2)
sns.boxplot(x='LaunchSite', y='PayloadMass', data=df)
plt.title('Payload Mass Range vs. Launch Site')

# 3. Success Rate vs. Orbit Type
plt.subplot(3, 2, 3)
orbit_success_rate = df.groupby('Orbit')['LandingOutcomeLabel'].mean()
sns.barplot(x=orbit_success_rate.index, y=orbit_success_rate.values)
plt.title('Success Rate vs. Orbit Type')
plt.ylabel('Success Rate')

# 4. Relationship between Successful Landing and Flight Number
plt.subplot(3, 2, 4)
sns.scatterplot(x='Flights', y='LandingOutcomeLabel', data=df)
plt.title('Successful Landing vs. Flight Number')

# 5. Success Rate of Payload Range for Each Orbit
plt.subplot(3, 2, 5)
sns.boxplot(x='Orbit', y='PayloadMass', data=df, hue='LandingOutcomeLabel')
plt.title('Payload Mass Range vs. Orbit Type')

# 6. Yearly Trend of Launch Success Rate
plt.subplot(3, 2, 6)
yearly_success_rate = df.groupby('Year')['LandingOutcomeLabel'].mean()
sns.lineplot(x=yearly_success_rate.index, y=yearly_success_rate.values, marker='o')
plt.title('Yearly Trend of Launch Success Rate')
plt.ylabel('Success Rate')

plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) Summary:¶

  1. Success Rate vs. Launch Site: The bar chart indicates that 'KSC LC 39A' has the highest success rate among the launch sites. This could be a good feature to consider for predicting landing success.

  2. Payload Mass Range vs. Launch Site: The boxplot shows a wide range of payload masses for each site, with 'CCSFS SLC 40' having the broadest range. There are some outliers in the payload masses at 'VAFB SLC 4E'.

  3. Success Rate vs. Orbit Type: Different orbits have varying success rates. For instance, ES-L1 and HEO have a 100% success rate, although they have fewer launches. GTO and ISS, which have more launches, also have good success rates.

  4. Successful Landing vs. Flight Number: The scatter plot doesn't show a clear trend between the number of flights and the success of the landing, but it's worth noting that most of the unsuccessful landings happen within the first few flights.

  5. Payload Mass Range vs. Orbit Type: The boxplot shows that the payload mass varies significantly depending on the orbit type. For example, GTO and ISS orbits can handle a wide range of payload masses.

  6. Yearly Trend of Launch Success Rate: The line graph suggests an increasing trend in the success rate over the years, particularly after 2013.

These insights will be very useful when we proceed to feature selection for our machine learning model. They also provide a quantitative perspective on the different aspects that could influence the landing success of a space mission.
